What Is Pediatric Sedation Dentistry?

Pediatric Sedation Dentistry involves the use of safe, carefully monitored medications to help children relax during dental treatments. Sedation can be mild, moderate, or deep—depending on your child’s needs and the type of procedure being performed. Our expert team ensures your child’s safety through every phase of the visit, using techniques approved by the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry.

Types of Sedation We Offer

Every child is unique, and so are their needs. That’s why our office offers several types of sedation to suit each child’s level of anxiety and the complexity of the procedure.

1.Nitrous Oxide Sedation (Laughing Gas): This is the most common and mildest form of sedation. It helps children feel relaxed and at ease while remaining awake and responsive. The effects wear off quickly, allowing children to return to normal activities soon after their appointment.

2.Oral Conscious Sedation: Ideal for children who need a deeper level of relaxation. This type of sedation is administered in liquid or pill form before treatment and helps keep your child calm throughout the procedure. They may feel drowsy but will remain conscious and able to respond.

3.IV Sedation: Typically reserved for longer or more complex procedures. It provides a deeper level of sedation and is carefully monitored by a specialist to ensure complete safety and comfort.

Our dentists will thoroughly explain each option to you, helping you make an informed decision that’s best for your child’s needs and comfort level.
